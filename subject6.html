What is the title of the first Sherlock Holmes book by Arthur Conan Doyle?

  • The Sign of the Four
  • A Case of Identity
  • The Doings of Raffles Haw
  • A Study in Scarlet
The correct answer is A Study in Scarlet.
"A Study in Scarlet" is the first Sherlock Holmes novel written by Sir Arthur Conan Doyle, first published in 1887.

George Orwell wrote this book, which is often considered a statement on government oversight.

  • The Old Man and the Sea
  • Catcher and the Rye
  • To Kill a Mockingbird
  • 1984
The correct answer is 1984.
George Orwell's "1984" is a dystopian novel that explores themes of government surveillance and totalitarianism.

Which famous book is sub-titled 'The Modern Prometheus'?

  • Dracula
  • The Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde
  • The Legend of Sleepy Hollow
  • Frankenstein
The correct answer is Frankenstein.
Mary Shelley's "Frankenstein" is subtitled "The Modern Prometheus" as it deals with themes of creation and the dangers of pushing beyond natural boundaries.
